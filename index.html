Tonight: La Réole

La Réole, one of the oldest towns along the Garonne, traces its roots to a powerful Benedictine priory founded in the 8th century. Its name derives from “Regula,” reflecting monastic rule. The town’s steep streets and arcaded square reveal medieval importance as a trading hub. Each summer, it hosts cultural festivals celebrating music and heritage. Quiet today, La Réole retains a sense of faded authority, with stone facades and river views marking its former strategic and religious significance.
